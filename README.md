In 2023, approximately 55.4% of U.S. children aged 6–17 (27.3 million) participated in organized sports or lessons. Participation is slightly higher for boys (56.1%) than girls (52%), with rates higher for children aged 12–17 (55.6%) compared to 6–11 (52.5%). Key benefits include improved physical health, reduced depression, better sleep, and enhanced social skills. 
Key Youth Sports Data Trends
Participation Drivers: Non-Hispanic White children (60.4%) show higher participation rates compared to Non-Hispanic Black (42.1%) and Hispanic (46.9%) children.
Frequency: About 24% of children participate in sports year-round, while 33% participate for 6–11 months.
Gender Differences: Boys more frequently engage in team sports (e.g., football), while girls often participate in individual/aesthetic sports (e.g., gymnastics, dance).
Activity Recommendations: Children require at least 60 minutes of daily physical activity, including three weekly sessions that strengthen muscles and bones.
Post-Pandemic Recovery: Nearly half of Hispanic youth (47%) and 42% of Black youth resumed sports at lower levels following the pandemic.
